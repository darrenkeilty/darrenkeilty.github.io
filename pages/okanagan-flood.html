Okanagan Lake Flood Dashboard

An interactive dashboard exploring the drivers and impacts of extreme lake levels

Project Overview

By Darren Keilty
CPSC 582 - Data Visualization Design and Development (Winter 2026)

This project is an interactive web-based visualization built with D3.js and Leaflet that explores high water years on Okanagan Lake. As a resident of the region for many years, I wanted to create coordinated views of lake level, snowpack, temperature, outflow, and shoreline impacts so viewers can contextualize recent flood events and understand the environmental conditions behind them.

The final dashboard is designed as a story-driven experience with five sections: a map for station and photo context, a ten-year lake-level comparison, a seasonal heatmap of full-pool exceedance, a multi-variable drivers view, and a shoreline impacts simulation. The goal is to help educate a general audience about the environmental conditions and consequences behind these events.

Key Highlights

  • Uses 2014 to 2024 daily source data from government hydrometric, climate, and snow-survey stations to ground the narrative in real observations.
  • Frames lake levels relative to full pool so high-risk months and years are easier to interpret at a glance.
  • Connects snowpack, spring warming, outflow, and lake level in a coordinated view to explain why high-water years like 2017 stand out.
  • Presents a shoreline house simulation to translate metre-level water changes into a more concrete residential flooding scenario.
