About This Visualization

The Combine Drills

40-Yard Dash lower = faster
The headline number. Players sprint 40 yards from a standing start. Measures pure straight-line speed and explosiveness off the line — critical for skill positions, and a surprising differentiator even for big linemen.
Vertical Jump higher = better
Players jump as high as possible from a standing position. Measures lower-body explosion and fast-twitch muscle power. Correlates heavily with athletic ability in receivers, defensive backs, and linebackers.
Broad Jump higher = better
A standing two-footed horizontal jump measured in inches. Tests explosive power and body control — athletes need to stick the landing. Often a better predictor of athleticism than the vertical for some positions.
Bench Press higher = better
225 lbs pressed as many times as possible. Measures upper-body strength and endurance. Matters most for linemen who need to hold blocks, but scouts track it for all positions as a raw strength baseline.
3-Cone Drill lower = faster
Three cones in an L-shape. Players sprint, cut, and change direction around them. One of the best measures of short-area agility and body control — particularly valued for pass rushers, receivers, and defensive backs.
Shuttle Run lower = faster
Also called the 20-yard short shuttle. Players go 5 yards right, 10 yards left, then 5 yards back. Tests lateral quickness and the ability to stop and accelerate — a key trait for linebackers and defensive backs in coverage.
